Critical choices

Why health care reform is so hard

As the future of health coverage in the United States remains up the air, we asked North Texans about their experiences with the Affordable Care Act — whom it helped, where it fell short, and what it cost.
The issues they raised show just how hard it is to reconcile the concerns of competing interests into a comprehensive national health care policy.
Some say the system needs to cover everyone, no matter how sick. Others were willing to forgo insurance and pay a penalty. Everyone agreed spiraling costs are a problem.
